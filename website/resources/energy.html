Energy (GCSE-level)

A system is a collection of mass and energy. This could be a moving car, a swimming pool, the Earth etc. Everything outside of the system is referred to as the surroundings
When analysing systems, the surroundings are defined relative to the system. For example if a car is moving right at 10 km/h (outside perspective), when it is being analysed (system perspective) it is considered to be stationary and the sourroundings are considered to be moving left at 10 km/h. These shifts in perspective can be useful when many things occur relative to a specific object.
An open system is one where mass or energy can leave or enter (for example a pool that is draining or a house losing heat due to an open window).
A closed system is one where mass or energy cannot leave or enter (for example an ideal hot water tank)
