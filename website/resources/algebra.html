Algebra

This topic assumes knowledge of negative numbers, addition and multiplication.

Operations

An operation is some logical action performed with one or two elements (in this case, numbers are elements), for example '1+2' is an addition operation between the elements 1 and 2. Most complex operations (involving more than two elements) can be broken down into a series of operations between two elements (binary operations).

Order of Operations

The order in which operations are applied affects the result. For example: doubling one then adding one is three; adding one then doubling that is four. Brackets isolate operations into groups. The contents of each group should be evaluated before applying external operations. The previous example can be written as $(1\times2)+1$ and $(1+1)\times2$, where the brackets show what step is carried out first.

Unknowns

Algebra is the mathematics of numbers that are not yet known. These unknown numbers are called variables and are represented by symbols (mainly letters). Variables can also be thought of as bracket groups, so if $a=1+1$ then $a\times2$ is the same as $(1+1)\times2$.
